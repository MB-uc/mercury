# CAPABILITY_LIBRARY.md
# Mercury Strategy — Capability archetype library
# Version 0.1 | March 2026

---

## Purpose

This library defines 16 named capability archetypes — recurring IDX
intervention types observed across client engagements. Each capability
maps to one or more website archetypes from ARCHETYPE_LIBRARY.md.

The Findings skill uses this library to translate High-confidence
archetype matches into implicit commercial signals. The connection is
made as a natural consequence of the diagnosis, not as an explicit
sales pitch.

### How to apply

1. For each High-confidence website archetype identified, look up the
   mapped capabilities below
2. Where a capability maps to two or more High-confidence archetypes
   simultaneously, treat it as a stronger signal — the capability
   addresses a systemic pattern, not an isolated gap
3. Surface capability connections in the Findings report as part of
   the "what this means" layer, not as a separate recommendations
   section
4. Never surface a capability connection for a Medium or Low-confidence
   archetype match

## Capability definitions

### C01 — Strategic content layer design

**Description:** IDX designs and builds the narrative architecture that
sits above tactical UX fixes — investment case pages, strategy hubs,
results highlight pages, and content structures that connect a company's
story to what specific audiences need to assess and act.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A01 — The document repository (primary)
- A02 — The strategy announcement without follow-through (primary)
- A05 — The foundational-only site (primary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site publishes diligently but does not package. Results are
accessible as raw documents; the investment case is absent; strategy
content exists but is not structured to be navigated or cited. The
missing layer is editorial — not more content, but a content
architecture that makes existing material work harder for each audience.

**Evidence clients:** Inchcape (global corporate site with regional
flexibility), ELC (Beauty Reimagined as a unifying thread across full
site structure).

**Outcome language:**
"Tactical fixes alone won't be enough. What's needed is a strategic
content layer that brings clarity, ambition, and a cohesive narrative
to the entire site experience."

### C02 — Audience architecture and multi-site structuring

**Description:** IDX defines the information architecture for sites
serving multiple distinct audiences — through segmented navigation,
tabbed microsites, or hybrid global/regional structures — so each
audience type is served directly without compromise to the others.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A03 — The invisible stakeholder (primary)
- A05 — The foundational-only site (secondary)
- A08 — The fragmented global presence (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site has multiple high-value audience types but treats them
identically. Investors, procurement teams, and talent candidates all
enter through the same homepage and navigate the same structure, with
no audience-specific pathways, content layers, or entry points. The
architecture reflects internal logic rather than user intent.

**Evidence clients:** St. James's Place (tabbed microsites for each
audience type), Orion Group (four brands unified under a single domain),
Inchcape (centralised control with local flexibility).

**Outcome language:**
"Each audience needs to feel the site was built for them. Right now it
was built for no one in particular."

### C03 — Conversion rate optimisation and funnel repair

**Description:** IDX audits the conversion funnel from traffic source
to action — identifying friction through heuristic analysis, heat
mapping, analytics review, and A/B testing — then runs iterative
experiments to improve conversion rates on key pages and landing
experiences.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A04 — The broken conversion funnel (primary)
- A13 — The B2B site built for linear journeys (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
Traffic is being delivered but not converted. Key pages lack clear
CTAs; the contact route is a single generic form regardless of audience
or intent; the application journey in careers exits the main site.
The gap is not awareness — it is the journey from arrival to action.

**Evidence clients:** Happy Valley (10% increase in engagement rate,
40% reduction in bounce rate on key pages), Biopelle (11.5% CVR
improvement, $200k+ additional revenue), Parker & Sons (1% CVR
improvement on 100k sessions modelled at $150k additional revenue).

**Outcome language:**
"The traffic exists. The intent exists. The question is why it isn't
converting — and that's answerable with the right data."

### C04 — Investor-facing content packaging

**Description:** IDX transforms static financial disclosures into
surfaced, contextualised, investor-ready content — results highlights
pages, investment case pages, interactive data, and narrative framing
that makes performance accessible without requiring document downloads.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A01 — The document repository (primary)
- A02 — The strategy announcement without follow-through (primary)
- A10 — The one-day website (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The IR section is a well-maintained document archive. Every filing is
present. But there is no investment case, no results highlights page,
no narrative layer connecting financial performance to strategic
delivery. Investors who do not already know the company well cannot
orient from the site alone.

**Evidence clients:** ELC IR (Beauty Reimagined narrative woven through
investor content), Inchcape (investment case as a core IA component).

**Outcome language:**
"Best-in-class companies don't just publish results — they package
them, pulling key highlights to the surface and connecting financial
performance to strategic delivery."

### C05 — Authority and visibility programme

**Description:** IDX builds the content, digital PR, and structured
data infrastructure that drives organic authority — research-led
campaigns, thought leadership, backlink development, and AI/search
visibility auditing — positioning the client as the cited source in
its category.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A05 — The foundational-only site (primary)
- A11 — The AI-invisible site (primary)
- A03 — The invisible stakeholder (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site is present in its category but not authoritative. Competitors
are cited by AI tools; this company is not. The newsroom is regulatory
announcements only. There is no thought leadership, no research content,
no structured data that would give an AI system something citable to
surface.

**Evidence clients:** Parker & Sons (brand mentions up 185%, total
reach up 182% in under three months), Diageo (consumer insights report
doubled earned coverage vs prior year).

**Outcome language:**
"Visibility in AI-mediated search is won by companies with quotable,
structured, specific content. Right now the site gives AI nothing
to work with."

### C06 — Analytics audit and tagging governance

**Description:** IDX audits GA4 configuration, event tracking,
conversion setup, and data validity, then rebuilds for accurate,
actionable measurement — including cross-domain tracking, conversion
event configuration, and integration with CRM and ATS data sources.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A04 — The broken conversion funnel (primary)
- A12 — The unintegrated multi-system estate (primary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site cannot answer basic questions about its own performance.
Conversion events are not configured; cross-domain tracking is broken
between the corporate site and the careers subdomain; the GA4
implementation does not reflect the current site architecture. Any
performance data being reported is unreliable.

**Evidence clients:** Jardines (GA4 misconfigured — no conversion events,
bounce rate data unreliable, events not optimised for GA4).

**Outcome language:**
"Before we can improve performance, we need to be able to measure it
accurately. Right now the data cannot be trusted."

### C07 — First-party data activation

**Description:** IDX connects web analytics, CRM, ATS, and paid media
into a unified data layer for full-funnel measurement — enabling
clients to understand true ROI across marketing, IR, and talent
acquisition investment.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A12 — The unintegrated multi-system estate (primary)
- A04 — The broken conversion funnel (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
Each platform generates its own data but none of it connects. The
corporate site, careers subdomain, and CRM operate independently.
There is no ability to track a journey from first visit to commercial
enquiry, from careers page to application, or from IR event to investor
contact. The estate is data-rich in silos and data-blind end-to-end.

**Evidence clients:** Swift/Knight Transportation (IDX built a data
engine connecting campaign tagging to ATS via API — result: -44% cost
per lead, -55% media spend, 8,000 resource hours saved annually).

**Outcome language:**
"The data exists. It just doesn't talk to itself. Connecting it would
transform your ability to make decisions about where to invest."

### C08 — ESG digital maturity audit

**Description:** IDX assesses the digital maturity of a company's ESG
web presence against a 100-point framework covering content structure,
data accessibility, technical implementation, and governance — producing
a benchmarked maturity score and prioritised improvement roadmap.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A01 — The document repository (secondary — ESG-specific)
- A05 — The foundational-only site (secondary — ESG-specific)
- A11 — The AI-invisible site (secondary — ESG-specific)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The ESG section publishes the right reports but does not make the data
accessible. ESG metrics are locked inside PDF documents; there is no
downloadable data pack; framework alignment is stated but not evidenced
with structured content. Rating agencies will find this section
frustrating; AI tools will find it uncitable.

**Evidence clients:** Internal IDX methodology, benchmarked against
Anglo American, BHP, Rio Tinto, Glencore, and 17 other peers.

**Outcome language:**
"ESG professionals — rating agencies, stewardship teams, regulators —
need structured data fast. A narrative sustainability report does not
serve them. An accessible, machine-readable ESG data hub does."

### C09 — AEO (answer engine optimisation) audit

**Description:** IDX benchmarks a client's visibility across AI answer
engines — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity — across three dimensions:
discoverability (AI share of voice by theme and query), authority
(citation patterns and content authority gaps), and readability
(structural and technical factors enabling AI extraction).

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A11 — The AI-invisible site (primary)
- A01 — The document repository (secondary — AI-specific)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
When stakeholders ask AI tools about this company's sector position,
capabilities, or ESG commitments, competitors are cited and this company
is not — or is cited with inaccurate or outdated information. The
content exists on the site but is not structured in a way that allows
AI systems to extract, attribute, and surface it confidently.

**Evidence clients:** Rolls-Royce AEO audit (March 2026) — benchmarking
AI share of voice vs GE Aerospace across aerospace, defence, energy,
and engineering themes.

**Outcome language:**
"AI answer engines generate responses based on topic association and
narrative strength, not web page ranking. The question is whether your
content gives them something specific enough to cite."

### C10 — Website business case and peer benchmarking

**Description:** IDX produces investment-grade internal business cases
with peer benchmarking data to help clients secure budget approval for
website redevelopment — quantifying business risk, commercial
opportunity, and competitive gap in terms that resonate with CFOs and
executive committees.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A06 — The legacy platform trap (primary)
- A05 — The foundational-only site (secondary)
- A07 — The invisible commercial audience (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The platform constraints and content gaps identified in this audit
represent a case for investment that needs to be made internally before
any improvement programme can begin. The benchmarking evidence gathered
here — peer comparison, audience gap analysis, platform risk signals —
is the raw material for that business case.

**Evidence clients:** Rolls-Royce website business case (January 2026)
— quantified platform risk (£130k/year maintenance, 6.9x traffic spike
vulnerability) and commercial gap (28% of traffic are customers, site
not set up to serve them), framing redevelopment as strategic necessity.

**Outcome language:**
"Before we can fix this, you need internal investment. We can help you
make that case with the evidence to support it."

### C11 — Careers platform design and technical delivery

**Description:** IDX designs and builds employer-brand-aligned careers
platforms including ATS integration, multilingual build, locale-
personalised content, faceted job search, and employer brand alignment
across all candidate touchpoints.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A09 — The careers site as afterthought (primary)
- A08 — The fragmented global presence (secondary — careers-specific)
- A12 — The unintegrated multi-system estate (secondary — ATS-specific)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The employer brand exists in internal documentation and recruitment
marketing but is not expressed in the digital careers experience.
The careers section is a functional placeholder — job search links to
an ATS on a separate subdomain, employee stories are absent, and the
design language is materially different from the corporate site.
Candidates comparing this company to peers will form a rapid and
unfavourable impression.

**Evidence clients:** Rolls-Royce careers platform (Cielo partnership)
— dynamic homepage, multilingual (EN/FR/DE), Workday ATS integration
across three business areas, advanced job search and filter, launched
on time with strong internal feedback.

**Outcome language:**
"The careers site is the first thing a candidate sees when they decide
to apply. Right now it is doing active damage to the employer brand
you have invested in building."

### C12 — CMD production and content campaign

**Description:** IDX delivers end-to-end Capital Markets Day production
— webcast platform (ConnectID), physical AV and staging, set design,
pre-event content, and post-event content atomisation into highlight
clips, quote graphics, write-ups, and social assets.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A10 — The one-day website (primary)
- A02 — The strategy announcement without follow-through (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
Capital markets events represent significant investment in management
time, preparation, and production. But the digital asset created for
those events disappears from the site once the event has passed.
Investors who could not attend live have no way to orient from the
digital estate; the materials that should be anchoring the investment
narrative are inaccessible.

**Evidence clients:** Heineken CMD 2025 (virtual platform, Seville
production), Rolls-Royce CMD 2023 (landing page, social, email, CEO
video, webcast, highlight video), Glanbia CMD 2022 (~100 LinkedIn
conversions from paid social).

**Outcome language:**
"The event is the beginning, not the end. The content it generates
should be working for you for months afterwards."

### C13 — AI readiness programme

**Description:** A structured four-component programme addressing AI
visibility at the content, brand, channel, and monitoring level:
AI Brand Hub (centralised structured data and brand voice), Content
Transformation Programme (restructuring existing content for AI
extraction), Community and Channel Optimisation, and AI Monitoring
System (ongoing share-of-voice tracking).

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A11 — The AI-invisible site (primary)
- A01 — The document repository (secondary — AI-specific packaging)
- A05 — The foundational-only site (secondary — AEO readiness)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site is not structured for the way audiences now research. AI
tools are increasingly the first point of contact for stakeholder
research — and right now this company is either absent from those
responses or represented inaccurately. An AI readiness programme
addresses this systematically, rather than treating AEO as a
one-time audit.

**Evidence clients:** Tetra Pak digital ecosystem proposal (April 2025)
— AI readiness identified as a core workstream given 20% of traditional
web traffic already affected by AI search summaries.

**Outcome language:**
"Twenty percent of web traffic is now AI-mediated. That proportion
will only increase. The question is not whether to prepare for this
— it is how quickly."

### C14 — Global digital estate re-platforming

**Description:** IDX migrates large multi-market web estates onto
Connect.ID PaaS — consolidating CMS, hosting, analytics, ATS, and
portal infrastructure across 50+ market sites into a governed,
scalable global platform with local flexibility.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A08 — The fragmented global presence (primary)
- A12 — The unintegrated multi-system estate (primary)
- A06 — The legacy platform trap (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The global estate has grown without governance. Market sites vary in
quality, design language, and content depth. There is no central
control over brand standards, no unified analytics, and no ability
to deploy content globally from a single CMS. The result is a digital
presence that reflects how the organisation has grown rather than how
it wants to be seen.

**Evidence clients:** Tetra Pak (55 market sites proposed migration to
Connect.ID), Inchcape (multi-market consolidation with regional
flexibility model).

**Outcome language:**
"What looks like a content problem is actually a platform problem.
You cannot create a consistent global experience from fragmented
infrastructure."

### C15 — B2B content strategy and buyer journey architecture

**Description:** IDX structures websites to serve non-linear B2B buying
processes — mapping journeys per audience type, designing site
architecture around buyer qualification and intent, and building the
content system that serves each stage of the decision cycle.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A13 — The B2B site built for linear journeys (primary)
- A07 — The invisible commercial audience (primary)
- A03 — The invisible stakeholder (secondary — commercial audience)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The site was designed for a visitor who arrives, reads, and enquires —
in that order. Real B2B buyers arrive multiple times over months,
at different stages of evaluation, with different questions at each
visit. The site cannot serve them at the orientation stage and the
technical evaluation stage simultaneously, so it fails both.

**Evidence clients:** Tetra Pak digital ecosystem proposal — B2B buyer
journey architecture identified as a primary workstream, with explicit
mapping of the non-linear procurement cycle across five decision stages.

**Outcome language:**
"B2B buyers don't follow a funnel. They circle. Your site needs to
be able to meet them wherever they are in that process."

### C16 — B2B lead capture and CRM infrastructure

**Description:** IDX designs and builds the back-end of B2B lead
generation — form-to-CRM flows, prospect database development, trial
request tracking, and Salesforce/HubSpot integration — connecting
digital engagement to measurable commercial pipeline.

**Addresses website archetypes:**
- A04 — The broken conversion funnel (primary)
- A12 — The unintegrated multi-system estate (secondary)
- A13 — The B2B site built for linear journeys (secondary)

**What the connection looks like in a Findings report:**
The commercial intent signals on this site — page visits, document
downloads, contact form submissions — are not connected to any CRM
system. There is no lead scoring, no pipeline visibility, no ability
to distinguish a high-value procurement prospect from a general
enquiry. Investment in driving traffic has no corresponding investment
in capturing and qualifying the commercial interest that traffic
generates.

**Evidence clients:** DeHavilland digital marketing and website
development RFP — B2B lead capture and CRM integration as a primary
technical requirement.

**Outcome language:**
"You're generating interest but not capturing it. Every untracked
visit from a procurement decision-maker is a commercial opportunity
you can't see."
